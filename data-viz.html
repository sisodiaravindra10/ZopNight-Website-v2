§ 13 · data visualization

Color is meaning.
Not decoration.

Every chart, table, gauge, and sparkline on zop.dev and inside the product uses the same color semantics. Blue is monitored (the thing we see). Orange is at risk (the thing that's wasting money). Green is reclaimed (the thing we fixed). If a chart uses orange for anything other than waste, it's wrong.

§ 01 · color semantics

Three colors. Three meanings.

The pillar colors carry semantic weight in every visualization. Don't invent your own mapping, the customer has already learned ours from the three-numbers dashboard.

01 · blue · #4A66D4

Monitored.

The total, the baseline, the thing you’re observing. Spent, resources counted, regions reached. Neutral, accountable. Used on dark canvas with the lifted blue.

§ 02 · three numbers

The signature pattern.

The three-numbers composition is the canonical way to open any page, report, or dashboard. Always in this order. Always with the 3px pillar strip on top. Always tabular-nums on figures.

Rules: · three columns, this order · always    · top 3px bar is the tell · don't skip it    · 48–72px figures depending on container    · tabular-nums on every figure    · never a fourth number

§ 03 · big number

Single metric, headline weight.

For a single figure on a card (proof tiles, case-study metric, pricing). Weight 700, tracking −0.04em, line-height 1. Follow with a mono caption and a 12-month context bar if relevant.

case-study metric 42% median week-1 cloud waste cut. Across 142 deployments.

§ 04 · sparklines

Tabular context, one row at a time.

Every resource row carries a 14-day trend sparkline. Color reflects the state: orange for wasteful drift, green for a resource that's stayed efficient, blue for stable-baseline. Delta shown as +/- percentage with tabular-nums.

resource $ / mo delta 14-day
web-prod · i-0abc1234 $842 -2.1%
analytics-rds · db.m5.2xl $1,204 +18%
worker-pool-7 · idle 14d $318 +0%
cache-redis-prod · terminated $0 -$390
Line color encodes state: blue = stable · orange = drifting up (waste growing) · green = drifting down (savings applied)
Shape: 80×24 viewBox · 1.5px stroke · no fills · no markers

§ 06 · gauges

Single-metric radial.

Gauges use SVG stroke-dasharray on a circle. Arc color follows the metric's semantic, orange for waste ratios, green for savings realized, blue for monitored counts. Thickness 8px stroke. Never full 360-ring, 270° arc minimum.

· waste ratio 26% of $182K monthly bill
· reclaimed 21% applied · rollback-ready
· uptime 99.98% 90-day · SLA threshold 99.9%

§ 07 · axis + grid

The rules behind every chart.

Axes, grid lines, tick marks. Never show what's noise.

axis line
1px solid var(--line) on x-axis only · no y-axis line · y-axis uses labels to their own left.
grid lines
horizontal only · 1px dashed var(--line) at rgba .5 · 4-5 lines max · never vertical grid.
tick labels
JetBrains Mono · 10px · var(--ink-4) · letter-spacing 0.04em · lowercase months (jan · feb · mar).
axis label
usually omitted, title of the chart makes it obvious. If kept: mono 10px uppercase 0.14em tracking.
units
money in thousands ($182K) · millions ($1.2M) · tabular-nums always. Never raw cents.
zero line
if chart crosses zero, draw a 1.5px var(--line-b) line at y=0. Labels above / below.
annotation
mono 10px label · thin 1px line pointing to the datum · offset 12px min clearance.
empty state
dashed outline of the chart container · centered mono label "no data in range".

§ 08 · time-series line

12-month spend trend.

The canonical chart for a cost platform — a thin line tracking monthly spend across a 12-month window, with a forecast band projecting the next 3 months. Annotation arrow at the point ZopDev kicked in. Tabular y-axis in thousands, lowercase month labels on x-axis.

monthly spend · aws + azure + gcp · 12-mo + 3-mo forecast
Rules: 2px stroke · solid for actual, dashed for forecast · 3px circle markers at data points · color shifts mid-line when ZopDev connects (blue → orange = "we changed the trajectory") · annotation line + mono label at inflection point.

§ 09 · multi-line

Breakdown by cloud vendor.

Three lines on one chart — AWS, Azure, GCP. Each gets its own pillar-adjacent tint. Legend beside the chart, never floating over it. Line weights uniform, no markers (too noisy with 3+ series).

spend by cloud · 12 weeks
aws $98K azure $58K gcp $26K
spend by team · top 3
platform data ml
Cloud-vendor lines use the pillar trio (orange / blue / green) because the vendors are the dominant dimension. Team lines use grayscale steps (ink / ink-3 / ink-5) when the team name is the dimension — avoids over-associating a team with a pillar color.

§ 10 · stacked area

Cost composition over time.

Stacked area answers: how is the spend distributed across categories, and how is that distribution shifting? Use when the total matters AND the breakdown matters. Order areas by value descending at the latest point.

spend composition · 12 months · by workload type
compute · $82K database · $52K storage · $32K network · $16K
Stacking order: largest at top, smallest at bottom. Opacity: 0.85 on pillar colors, 0.65 on cream (so it still reads on dark). Never overlap areas with different meanings — the reader should always be able to trace one band horizontally.

§ 11 · horizontal bar

Top-N resources by spend.

The “worst offenders” view. Sort descending by value. Resource name on the left, money on the right, bar fills the track proportional to max. Red bar = non-remediated, orange = in-queue, green = just-remediated.

Bar colors are semantic: orange = needs attention (high spend or idle), blue = healthy running, green = recently terminated (display for 30 days with strike-through name + negative delta). Never a rainbow of bars just because the categories differ.

§ 12 · treemap

Cost hierarchy at a glance.

Treemap answers: how much of the pie is each account, and inside each account, each service? Every rectangle is a spend tier. Size = monthly $. Color = cloud vendor. Hover = full path.

cost hierarchy · account → service · april 2026
Rectangles always border each other with 2px ink. Never rounded, never gapped. Color = the top-level dimension (cloud vendor). Opacity = depth (higher opacity = higher level in the tree). Text contrast: dark text on orange/green, cream text on blue.

§ 13 · waterfall

Where the money actually went.

A waterfall chart shows the bridge from spend last month to spend this month — accumulating each change. Orange bars add (new resources, scale-ups), green bars subtract (remediations, rightsizing). Start and end totals are ink. Critical for Showback reports.

march → april spend bridge · how we got from $182K to $152K
total added saved

§ 14 · anomaly detection

Highlighting the spike.

Any chart can show an anomaly. The band is the expected range (7-day p50 ± 1 std). The line is the actual. The red dot + leader + label appears only at the datum that breached. Never highlight "maybe" spikes — the callout is for definite anomalies only.

eu-west-2 egress · 14 days · anomaly detected apr 22

§ 15 · event timeline

Remediation history.

A timeline shows what happened, when, by whom. Every event is a horizontal row with mono timestamp, pillar-tinted icon, short subject line, and optional $ delta. Used in audit log, Showback reports, incident post-mortems.

remediation timeline · last 24 hours
14:32 utc Terminated worker-pool-7 · idle 14d · by CDCR auto-apply +$318/mo
13:15 utc Right-sized analytics-rds: db.m5.2xl → db.t3.medium · by ravindra +$1,480/mo
11:42 utc Scheduled dev-eu staging · mon-fri 08-19 GMT · by policy +$2,140/mo
09:20 utc Flagged 14 idle EC2 instances · us-east-1 · awaiting approval +$2,810/mo
00:00 utc CDCR scan run #12,847 · 847 resources · 12 new findings · 0 regressions —
Dot color: green = remediation applied · orange = finding flagged · blue = schedule/policy change · cream = system event (scan, maintenance). Timeline runs oldest at bottom, newest at top (reverse chronological — like a chat feed).

§ 17 · kpi scorecard

Multi-metric at a glance.

A scorecard shows 6–12 metrics side-by-side, each with its value, delta, and direction. Used as an exec summary row at the top of a report. Uniform cards, no oversized metric.

resources 3,247 ↑ +124
spent $182K ↓ -9%
wasted $47K ↓ -18%
reclaimed $38K ↑ +24%
remediations 47 ↑ +12
uptime 99.98% • within sla
Delta color-coding: positive-for-outcome = green · negative-for-outcome = orange. A rising waste number is bad (orange). A rising reclaimed number is good (green). The direction arrow is informational; the color is the judgment.

§ 18 · enforcement

Do · Don’t.

DO
  • Map blue = monitored · orange = at risk · green = reclaimed, every time
  • Use tabular-nums on every figure, prices, percentages, counts
  • Sparklines at 1.5px stroke, 80×24 viewBox, no fills
  • Heatmaps with single-color alpha ramp, six stops
  • Gauges as 270°-max arcs, 8px stroke, butt caps
  • Mono for tick labels, month names lowercase
  • Horizontal grid only, dashed, at most 5 lines
  • Annotation with thin 1px leader lines + mono labels
DON'T
  • No chart-color palette beyond the three pillars + ink
  • No two-color diverging maps · no rainbow scales
  • No filled area charts with gradients
  • No pie charts at all, use big-number or bar chart
  • No 3D effects · no drop shadows on data bars
  • No vertical grid lines · no ambient watermarks
  • No rounded line-caps on strokes
  • No chart junk · no legends longer than 3 items
