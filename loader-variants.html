// loader variants · pick one to ship

Ten loaders. Pick one.

Brand-coherent loader concepts in the ZopDev system, sharp corners, hard offsets, restrained palette, no gradients. Each loops continuously below so you can watch the cadence before choosing.

L1 · TETRIS DROP
Pieces fall and assemble the mark
Three brand-colour squares fall from above, staggered 300ms apart, settle into the 2×2 mark layout, then exit upward to loop.
ZopDevZopDev
L2 · WORDMARK SWEEP
Brand colour wipes through the wordmark
Ink wordmark · orange overlay clipped left-to-right via clip-path · 2s cycle, no easing on the wipe so the line stays sharp.
L3 · IRIS COLLAPSE
Paper square scales down to reveal the mark
Most architectural reading, the loader feels like a shutter opening on the mark. Sharp scale, no rotation.
L4 · DIRECTIONAL ASSEMBLY
Squares slide in from 3 directions
Blue from the left, orange from the top, green from the bottom · they snap into the mark layout, hold, then exit the way they came.
ZopDev
L5 · TERMINAL TYPE
Wordmark types in with mono caret
Letter-by-letter reveal in 7 steps · blinking orange caret stays visible after the wordmark settles. Most operator/CLI feeling option.
L6 · MARK ORBIT
Three squares orbit a centre dot
Constant 1.6s rotation · ink centre dot anchors the orbit · the three brand colours stay equidistant. Most kinetic option.
L7 · PULSE TRIO
Three brand dots pulse in sequence
Smallest visual footprint · sequential pulse, 150ms offset between dots · the simplest, "always-watching" choice.
L8 · STACK BUILD
Bars rise to form the mark grid
Blue tall bar fills first · orange + green half-bars stack right · transform-origin from bottom. Reads as "loading the structure."
L9 · SCAN LINE
Horizontal beam reveals the mark
Orange beam sweeps top-to-bottom · clip-path follows behind it, painting the mark in. Clean technical/scanner energy.

// diagonal arrow set · stairs / climb / sweep

Three blocks. Climbing up-right.

Five concepts where the brand-mark squares read as a diagonal arrow ascending, the universal "going up" signal for a cost-cutting / capacity-rising / compute-scaling product.

D1 · CONTINUOUS GLIDE
Locked stair slides up-right
Three-block stair stays locked together as one rigid unit and glides up-right at constant speed · fades out at the top, restarts at the bottom · the simplest "going up" reading.
D2 · RATCHETED STEPS
Snaps to discrete positions
Stair unit snaps cleanly to four positions · CSS `steps(4)` timing function · each snap is sharp, no easing in between. Reads as "ratcheting up the staircase, one rung at a time."
D3 · COMPRESS & LAUNCH
Anticipate, then climb
Stair briefly compresses (0.86 × 1.05), springs to anticipation (1.05 × 0.92), then launches up-right · the only variant with a coiled-spring physical reading. Sharp scales, no bounce easing.
D4 · TRAILING GHOSTS
Stair leaves fading copies behind
The locked stair travels up-right and leaves two ink-toned ghost copies behind it · ghosts run the same animation 180ms and 360ms behind the lead unit · richest motion read.
D5 · TRACKED CLIMB
Travels along a dotted track
A dotted diagonal track sets the path · the locked stair travels along it · an orange tip-marker pulses at the destination as the unit arrives. Most explicit "going up" semantics.
