01The problem

Zopping is a multi-tenant e-commerce platform serving 4,000+ retailers. Their engineering team had grown from 12 to 80 in two years. The cloud bill had grown faster — from $14k/mo to $71k/mo, with no proportional jump in revenue. Most of the increase was in non-production environments.

The platform team had run a manual audit twice and proposed scheduling rules each time. Both proposals stalled in review for weeks because nobody wanted to be the one who shut down a teammate's environment by mistake. By the time someone could approve, the proposed rules were already out of date.

"We knew the savings were there. The problem was the workflow." — Aditi Sharma, VP Engineering

  • 14 AWS accounts, 3 GCP projects, no consolidated cost view
  • Non-prod environments running 24/7 across 6 timezones
  • Right-sizing untouched — every service still on the instance class it was launched with 18 months ago
  • Orphan EBS volumes, snapshots, and NAT gateways nobody owned

02The solution

Zopping connected ZopNight in under 5 minutes with read-only IAM. The first scan completed in 90 seconds and surfaced findings worth $52k/mo across the 14 AWS accounts. They reviewed the plan for two days, then enabled scheduling on dev and staging environments — production stayed armed and untouched.

The platform team chose a phased rollout:

  • Day 1–3: Read-only inventory across all accounts. Owners review the proposed scheduling rules in Slack.
  • Day 4–7: Scheduling enabled on dev environments. Friday off, weekend dark, Monday 8am restore. Dependency-aware so DBs come up before app servers.
  • Day 8–14: Right-sizing recommendations applied to over-provisioned instances. T3.xlarge → t3.medium where utilization was <15%.
  • Day 15–30: Orphan cleanup runs nightly. Unused snapshots and unattached volumes deleted with 7-day grace.

The platform team didn't write a single Terraform change for the schedules — they live as ZopNight rules and version-control alongside the rest of the IaC repo. Override is a Slack command for any team that needs an environment up off-hours.

03The results

Within 30 days Zopping was saving $48,000/mo. The bigger surprise was the time savings: the platform team estimates they got back ~6 hours per week previously spent on cost-review meetings and ad-hoc shutdowns. "The thing that finally made this work," Aditi said, "was that nobody had to feel responsible for breaking someone else's environment. The schedule did it. If anyone needed it back up, they hit a Slack command. No friction, no blame."

"We had 14 AWS accounts with no consolidated view. ZopNight showed us everything wrong in the first scan."— Aditi Sharma · VP Engineering · Zopping

The platform team has since extended ZopNight rules to a 4th cloud account they acquired through M&A and is piloting the auto-remediation track for security findings. They publish a monthly savings report to the board now, sourced directly from ZopNight's showback exports.
