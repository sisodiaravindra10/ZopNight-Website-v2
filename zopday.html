faq

Things teams ask before they sign.

How does ZopDay compare to our existing Terraform setup? +

Terraform models infrastructure. ZopDay models the platform on top of it: the landing zone, the deploy pipeline, the drift remediation, the audit trail, the one-click rollback. You can keep your Terraform and run ZopDay alongside it; or you can use ZopDay templates as the starting modules.

Either way, ZopDay never hides what's on your cloud. Every resource it creates is inspectable in your AWS, GCP, or Azure console: same names, same tags, same APIs. If you ever leave, the workloads stay where they are.

Do ZopDay templates replace Terraform? +

No. Templates are opinionated platform blueprints: a stack (e.g. Node + Postgres + Redis) wired with the deploy pipeline, secrets layout, observability, and audit trail you need on day one. They sit on top of Terraform-style infrastructure, not in place of it.

For greenfield projects you can let a template stand up the underlying landing zone via ZopDay, and it'll emit the equivalent Terraform so you keep an IaC source of truth. For existing estates, point ZopDay at the cloud account; it reads what's there and lets you graft templates onto live infrastructure without rewrites.

Can I bring my own Terraform modules? +

Yes. Point ZopDay at a Terraform module in a Git repo or registry; ZopDay calls it the same way your CI would, captures the plan output for review, applies with scoped credentials, and forwards the audit log to your SIEM. State stays in your backend (S3, GCS, Azure Blob, Terraform Cloud), not ours.

This is how teams with existing IaC investments adopt ZopDay incrementally: keep your modules, gain the platform layer (deploy pipeline, drift detection, RBAC, DORA metrics) without re-authoring infra code.

How does drift remediation interact with our existing IaC? +

ZopDay watches your cloud accounts directly (not your Terraform state) so drift is detected against real resources, not your last apply. When something drifts (an IAM policy widened in the console, a security group manually edited, an untagged resource provisioned by a script) ZopDay opens a PR-style remediation against the IaC source, with the diff, the actor, and the timestamp.

You decide whether to auto-fix safe categories (tags, idle cleanup, certs) or hold all writes for human approval. Production writes are always opt-in, scoped per resource type, and fully logged.

Do we need to give up our existing CI? +

No. ZopDay plugs into GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, CircleCI, or Buildkite. You keep your build steps; ZopDay handles the rollout, secrets injection, ingress, cert-manager, and audit log. If you don't have a CI yet, the GitHub App ships a working pipeline on day one.

What happens to our workloads if we leave ZopDay? +

They keep running. ZopDay is a control plane, your workloads live in your AWS, GCP, or Azure account, deployed via Helm into your Kubernetes clusters, with secrets in your cloud's native vault. If you turn ZopDay off, nothing in the data path changes. You lose the ZopDay UI, the drift remediation, and the audit forwarding, but the application keeps serving traffic.

Can we bring our own Helm charts? +

Yes. Point ZopDay at a chart in a repo, an OCI registry, or your existing chart museum. ZopDay applies your values overlays, your ingress class, and your secret refs without rewriting the chart.

What's the security model? +

BYOC. Your workloads run on your cloud account; ZopDay never hosts them. Secrets sit in your cloud's native secret manager, never ours. Application traffic never traverses ZopDay, we are a control plane, not a proxy. Production write actions are opt-in, scoped per role, and fully logged. ISO 27001:2022, SOC 2 Type II, IRDAI mapped, DPDP DPA available.
