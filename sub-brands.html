§ 09 · sub-brand system

One family.
Three products.

ZopDev is the umbrella. ZopNight, ZopDay, ZopCloud sit under it. Each inherits the type, the motion, and the voice — but each owns one of the three pillar colors as its primary identity. Blue belongs to ZopNight. Orange belongs to ZopDay. Green belongs to ZopCloud. Never swap.

§ 01 · the umbrella

ZopDev holds all three pillars.

The parent brand uses the full three-tile mark at the top-right of its canonical lockup. All three pillars present together — nothing privileged, nothing hidden. When the umbrella speaks, it speaks for the whole family.

ZopDev never appropriates one pillar. If you see the ZopDev lockup, it belongs to the platform — the Continuous CloudOps story, the pricing page, the trust center, the homepage. Not a single sub-product.

§ 02 · pillar binding

Three pillars.
Three lanes.

Each pillar is claimed by exactly one sub-brand. This binding is locked — never use ZopNight in orange. Never use ZopDay in green. The color is the identity, not decoration.

When to use which.

If you’re building a page or an asset, decide the sub-brand first. The color, the voice, and the motion all follow from that decision — don’t pick them independently.

use ZopNight when
  • The surface is about non-prod scheduling · turning things off
  • The surface is about idle detection · sprawl, waste discovery
  • The audience is infra / SRE teams optimizing quiet hours
  • The voice is clinical, calm — systems sleeping
  • The outcome is "the cloud bill went quiet"
use ZopDay when
  • The surface is about deployment · CI/CD · release velocity
  • The surface is about pipelines · canary · feature flags
  • The audience is platform / eng teams shipping daily
  • The voice is energetic, direct — “ship it”
  • The outcome is “shipped 47 times this week”
use ZopCloud when
  • The surface is about reporting · Showback · attribution
  • The surface is about savings · the reclaimed number
  • The audience is FinOps / CFO teams defending spend
  • The voice is precise, tabular — numbers punctuated
  • The outcome is “$48,210 recovered this quarter”

Sub-brand rules.

The bindings below are not suggestions. If a PR violates a “don’t,” it gets rejected at code review — the grammar of the family has to hold.

DO
  • Lock each sub-brand to one pillar color (blue / orange / green)
  • Use the umbrella ZopDev lockup for platform-level pages (home, pricing, trust)
  • Let the sub-brand pick its voice within the brand voice (clinical / energetic / precise)
  • Keep the 32×32 mark viewBox for all three sub-brands
  • Download the SVG from the official pack — never redraw
  • Give sub-brand wordmarks a 1:1 gap-to-mark ratio in lockups
DON'T
  • Mix sub-brand pillars (no ZopNight in orange · no ZopDay in green)
  • Put the ZopDev full-color mark on a sub-brand page
  • Rotate · skew · recolor any sub-brand mark
  • Add a fourth sub-brand without brand-owner approval
  • Use sub-brand marks in ZopDev umbrella materials
  • Animate the sub-brand mark beyond the ZopDev motion signature
  • Pair sub-brand mark with any wordmark other than its own
