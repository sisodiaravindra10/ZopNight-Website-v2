State of Cloud Waste 2026.

We analysed 4,200 production accounts across AWS, GCP, and Azure. Non-prod runs 24/7 in 71% of them. Right-sizing alone left $312M on the table this year. The full per-pattern breakdown, what works, what doesn't.

Format PDF · Pages 64 · Read ~28 min · Released Apr 2026
Get the free PDF

No card. No drip campaign. One email with the PDF and a one-pager summary.

what you'll learn

Four findings that will change how you budget Q3.

Every section is data-backed, drawn from anonymised customer accounts. No vendor handwaving.

01

Where the 58% goes

The five categories of non-prod waste, ranked by dollar size. Idle instances are #2 — what's #1 will surprise you (it surprised us).

02

Right-sizing actually pays

The 30–50% savings we measured per over-provisioned resource, broken down by family. T3, M5, R5, and the burstable trap that costs more than it saves.

03

Orphans are the easy money

$50–300/mo per account in unattached EBS volumes, forgotten snapshots, idle NAT gateways. Day-one savings, no risk.

04

What scheduling actually returns

The 70% reduction we see in non-prod, broken down by environment type and team size. Spoiler: smaller teams save more.

by the numbers

The four numbers that kept showing up.

Each one is the median across all 4,200 accounts. The full distribution per category is in the report.
