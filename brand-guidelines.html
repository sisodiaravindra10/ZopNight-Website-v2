chapter 01 · the story

We rebuilt the brand around one emotion.

Seven interviews. Two workshops. One empathy map. One word-frequency analysis. The research all pointed to the same thing: engineering and FinOps leaders don't lose sleep over saving money. They lose sleep over being asked a question they can't answer.

The CFO asks why AWS grew 28% last quarter. The board review is Friday. A new CTO is walking in on Monday. ZopDev is the thing that makes sure the answer is on-screen before the question gets asked.

Three pillars came out of the research.

Not three features. Three feelings the brand has to deliver, in that order, every time:

  1. Secure, visibility, trust, the defensible posture. Blue carries this.
  2. Speed, the pace at which you find what's wrong and fix it. Orange carries this.
  3. Savings, the outcome, the number at the end. Green carries this.

"Cloud cost is an anxiety product. Savings is the outcome. The trigger is accountability."

– research synthesis · Q1 2026 · see /research-emotion.html

Everything in this document, the mark, the palette, the type, the motion, the voice, exists to serve that emotional truth. If a new design decision doesn't help an engineering leader feel more defensible, it doesn't belong in the brand.

research sources: 7 interview transcripts · 2 workshop outputs · empathy map · word-frequency report · JTBD framework · trigger-event log · competitor Venn · archived in /research/ (internal).
chapter 02 · the three pillars

Secure. Speed. Savings.

Three pillars. Three colors. One order, always. The order encodes the emotional arc the customer travels: first they feel safe (secure), then they feel fast (speed), then they see the number (savings).

01 · secure
Secure.
visibility · trust · defensibility · the posture a CTO can stand behind in a board meeting. Blue carries it.
#2A4494
02 · speed
Speed.
pace of discovery · pace of action · the accent color · 5 min to connect · 7 min to first fix · orange carries it.
#F58549
03 · savings
Savings.
the outcome · the number at the end · the reclaimed budget · the "recoverable" column · green carries it.
#7FB236

Rules

  • The pillar → color binding is locked. Blue is Secure. Orange is Speed. Green is Savings. Never swap.
  • When all three appear together (e.g. on the three-numbers dashboard), the order is always blue · orange · green, left to right.
  • The three-numbers dashboard on the homepage encodes this order in UI: Spent (blue) → Wasted (orange) → Recoverable (green). The composition is the identity.
chapter 03 · the mark

Wordmark first. Mark off-aligned.

The primary lockup reads ZopDev on the left, Space Grotesk, heavy weight, custom-drawn. A three-tile mark sits off-aligned to the top-right, forming an L-shape (blue top-left, orange top-right, green bottom-right). The empty bottom-left tile is deliberate, it's the void the product fills. Sharp corners. No gradients. Never rotated. Never redrawn.

Clear space + minimum size

Clear space Equal to the width of one tile (x) on every side. Nothing, no copy, no line, no other mark, encroaches inside this boundary.

Minimum size · lockup 72px wide on screen. Never smaller than that, the wordmark breaks down.

Minimum size · standalone mark 20px on color backgrounds, 16px in monochrome. Below 16px the empty bottom-left tile reads ambiguous, switch to the monochrome mark.

Lockup anatomy

The lockup is a single drawn artwork, not a mark and wordmark composed at runtime. The SVG symbol #logo-zopdev contains both elements with the correct spatial relationship baked in. Never recompose from DOM tiles + text.

chapter 04 · color system

Five brand colors. Three statuses. No more.

Every color the system uses is either a brand pillar (carries emotion) or a status semantic (carries information). Never mix, a pillar color never means "error," a status color never means "savings."

Usage ratios on the dark canvas

The homepage runs on #060608. Approximate surface distribution:

  • ~70%, Ink (cream text) on bg-0/bg-1 surfaces. The reading surface.
  • ~20%, Elevation surfaces (bg-2, bg-3) for cards and panels.
  • ~5%, Orange. Primary CTA. The "Wasted" figure. The accent.
  • ~3%, Blue. "Spent." Secure-pillar meta. Featured metrics.
  • ~2%, Green. "Recoverable." Savings-pillar meta. Success states.

Orange is the accent. Never more than one orange moment per viewport. Over-using it collapses the hierarchy, the eye stops knowing where to land.

– rule · enforced at code review

One exception, the logo-only lifted blue

The canonical brand blue #2A4494 is a deep navy. Against the #060608 dark canvas, once it becomes a large filled tile sitting next to the brighter orange and green siblings, it reads muted and the three tiles lose visual balance. A single-purpose lifted blue #4A66D4 is used only inside the logo and standalone-mark SVGs when they render on dark surfaces, never anywhere else, never as a brand color. Applied via the --logo-blue CSS variable (default #2A4494, override #4A66D4 on dark).

chapter 05 · typography

Two faces. No substitutes.

Space Grotesk, display, body, UI. Weights 300 through 700. JetBrains Mono, numbers, meta labels, code, timestamps. That's the entire type system.

Scale

Eleven steps. Stop exactly on one. Never invent a 54px or 40px, pick t-48 or t-52.

t-96 · hero headline max
clamp() at the top of the scale, -0.05em tracking
t-72 · three-number figure
tabular-nums always, pillar-colored per slot
t-60 · section heading max
h2 default at wide viewports
t-48 / t-44 · proof + pricing figures
always tabular-nums
t-28 / t-24 · card headlines + h3
weight 600–700
t-20 · hero lead
max-width 720px · line-height 1.45
t-17 · section lede
body tone, ink-3 color
t-15 · body
default reading size, ink-2
t-13 · UI default
nav links · buttons · labels
t-11 / t-10 · mono meta
uppercase 0.14em ls, always JetBrains Mono

Signature rules

  • Letter-spacing baseline: -0.02em everywhere. Display ≥64px drops to -0.05em.
  • Tabular-nums: every figure, prices, percentages, timestamps, uses font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums. No exceptions.
  • Mono for meta: uppercase labels, section tags, table headers, counters, status chips, are JetBrains Mono at 10–11px with letter-spacing: 0.14em. No decorative prefixes of any kind.
  • Weight ladder: 300 (rare) · 400 (body) · 500 (UI-strong) · 600 (card h3) · 700 (h1, h2, large numbers). Never bold a body paragraph.
chapter 06 · motion

Motion is the product, working.

The motion system exists to make a static page feel like telemetry you can watch. Every animation is functional, it signals that something is happening, not that the page is decorated.

The signature

One hover behavior defines the whole system:

  • translateY(-4px), the card rises 4px
  • border-color → ink, the border brightens to cream
  • box-shadow: 0 8px 0 -4px var(--zop-orange), a hard orange block appears 8px below, no blur
  • ::after bar grows top-to-bottom in orange, 4px wide, along the left edge

All four changes fire at the same 250ms ease-out. That single gesture is the feel of ZopDev.

Durations

--dur-fast · 160ms
hover state-change · arrow slide · link underline
--dur-med · 250ms
card lift · border-brighten · CTA fill-slide · the signature
--dur-slow · 400ms
count-up reveal · three-numbers stagger · hero entrance
--dur-xslow · 520ms
section reveal on viewport enter · only this one use

Eases

Four cubic-beziers. Never invent a fifth.

  • --ease-out · cubic-bezier(.22, 1, .36, 1) · default for all state changes
  • --ease-in-out · cubic-bezier(.65, 0, .35, 1) · subtle loops (mark pulse)
  • --ease-spring · cubic-bezier(.175, .885, .32, 1.275) · only for reveal-in
  • --ease-snap · cubic-bezier(.19, 1, .22, 1) · rare · close-in accelerations

Respect prefers-reduced-motion, always. The system collapses to a 1ms no-op globally when the user has the setting on.

– baseline · enforced in tokens.css
chapter 07 · voice & tone

Direct. Declarative. Confident.

Short sentences. Active verbs. Concrete numbers. No hedging. No "solutions"-speak. Every sentence either tells the reader what to do or what they'll feel when they do it.

Principles

  • Outcome over feature. Lead with the number the customer cares about, not the feature that produced it.
  • Claim language. We don't enter categories, we name them. "The Continuous CloudOps platform." "The CDCR loop." "The three-numbers dashboard."
  • Numbers are punctuation. Every claim is immediately followed by a concrete figure. "5 min to connect. 7 min to first fix. 42% median week-one cut."
  • Em dashes over colons. The rhythm of the brand.

Get ready for your next board review with confidence, ZopDev helps you prepare.

Words we use

see · find · fix · continuous · defensible · reversible · loop · under 60 seconds · the only · one click

Words we never use

solutions · leverage · synergy · best-in-class · enterprise-grade (unless literally stating a tier) · empower · journey · unlock value

chapter 09 · sub-brands

One family, three products.

ZopDev is the umbrella. ZopNight, ZopDay, and ZopCloud sit under it. Each inherits the mark, type, and motion, but each owns one of the three pillar colors as its primary identity color.

ZopDev · parent
all three pillar colors · dark canvas default · the Continuous CloudOps platform
ZopNight · blue · secure
cloud optimization at night, when systems sleep. Blue is dominant.
ZopDay · orange · speed
deployment + release velocity. Orange is dominant.
ZopCloud · green · savings
managed savings + Showback reporting. Green is dominant.

Rules for sub-brand marks

  • Each sub-brand uses the same 2×2 grid structure. The product's pillar tile is full saturation; the other two tiles drop to var(--bg-3) (on dark) or rgba(10,10,10,0.06) (on paper).
  • Wordmark: "Zop" stays in ink/cream · the product suffix ("Night", "Day", "Cloud") takes the pillar color.
  • Never use ZopDev's full-color mark on a sub-brand page. Pick a lane.
chapter 10 · standing rules

Enforced at every code review.

These are not suggestions. A PR that violates any of the "don't" rules below gets rejected on principle, the grammar of the brand has to hold, even when no one's looking.

DO
  • Solid color fills · pillar-tinted rgba for subtle surfaces
  • Hard offset shadows: 0 8px 0 -4px var(--zop-orange)
  • Hairline 1px borders · sharp corners globally
  • One orange moment per viewport
  • Tabular-nums on every figure
  • Mono for meta · Sans for everything else
  • Respect prefers-reduced-motion
  • Pillar → color binding (blue=secure · orange=speed · green=savings)
  • Three-numbers order always: Spent · Wasted · Recoverable
DON'T
  • No radial-gradient anywhere, especially no faded orange / blue / green "glow" blobs on hero or CTA sections. Banned outright.
  • No linear-gradient on components. Only exception: the homepage body pixel-grid wash at rgba(255,255,255,0.025).
  • No filter:blur. Backdrop-filter permitted only on the sticky homepage nav, nowhere else.
  • No drop-shadow with any blur radius
  • No rounded corners (9999px pills are the only exception)
  • No two pillar colors on the same CTA
  • No rotation or skew on the mark
  • No fourth tile on the mark
  • No "leverage" / "solutions" / "synergy" copy
  • No // decorative prefix on labels · no code-comment cosplay anywhere, ever
  • No figure without tabular-nums
